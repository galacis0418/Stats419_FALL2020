3. The Iris dataset is a multivariate dataset invented by a Biologist names Ronald Fisher in the 1930's.  He was a biologist and 
was using the data to understand the differences between the three variations of iris flowers.  There are 150 samples total with
a third from each species. The Species are Versicolor, virginica, and Setosa. The data he collected was the length and width of the petals.
This data is still used today, but mainly for statistics.
